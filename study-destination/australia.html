Australia

Australia is a top destination for international students, attracting over 690,000 in 2018—a number that grew by 11% from the previous year. With more than 1,100 institutions and 22,000 courses, its globally recognized education system offers diverse study options including universities, vocational training, and English programs.

Ranked second globally for international study, Australia features seven cities in the world’s top 100 for students. Lower tuition fees and living costs, part-time work opportunities, and scholarships make it a more affordable option than the US or UK, while its English-speaking environment ensures easier adaptation.

Why Choose for Higher Education

Several top global institutions have campuses in Australia, offering a wide range of internationally recognized degrees and courses. The Australian education system is known for its strong emphasis on academic excellence, innovation, and research. As a result, Australian graduates are highly sought after by employers worldwide. The country’s multicultural society also enhances the student experience by offering a diverse and enriching cultural environment.

What Is Go8?

Australia's eight leading research-focused universities form the Group of Eight (Go8), established in 1999. This alliance comprises the country’s most prestigious institutions, known for their academic excellence and impactful research.

Admission Requirements:

Applying to Australian universities is straightforward, but students should carefully manage their documentation and the GST process. Most universities have similar basic admission requirements for international students, which typically include:

  •  A copy of a valid passport Official academic records, such as transcripts and certificates
  •  recommendation letters
  •  A summary of your professional background, if applicable
  •  English language testing: PTE or IELTS
  •  Specific Mission statement

Cost to Study In Australia

Australia’s top-tier universities and high-quality education system make it a preferred destination for international students. However, tuition fees vary depending on the institution and course, with undergraduate programs typically costing between 12,000 and 15,000 AUD per semester.

In addition to tuition, students must budget for living costs—including accommodation, food, and transport—which average around 21,041 AUD per year. Travel expenses may add up to 2,200 AUD. A key advantage is that international students can work up to 24 hours per week, allowing them to gain experience and help cover their living and travel costs.

Scholarship:

Australia’s high-quality education system and diverse cultural environment make it a top choice for international students. While tuition costs can be a concern, various scholarships are available—some covering up to 30% of tuition and living expenses—making education in Australia more accessible and affordable.

Work Opportunities for Students:

International students who wish to remain in Australia after completing their studies benefit from excellent post-study work opportunities. After earning a bachelor’s degree, they can apply for a Post-Study Work (PSW) visa, allowing them to stay and work in Australia for two to four years, depending on their course duration.

Similarly, master's degree graduates may qualify for a PSW visa lasting three to five years. These visas enable students to gain valuable work experience, earn income, and advance their careers. Australia’s strong post-study work options make it an attractive destination for students looking to build a future after graduation.

Visa Requirements

To study in Australia, you’ll need a Student Visa (subclass 500). Here’s a brief overview of the key requirements:

General specifications:

Enrolled in a registered course

To be eligible under the Commonwealth Register of Institutions and Courses for Overseas Students (CRICOS), you must be enrolled full-time in a registered course.

Requirements for Genuine Temporary Entrants (GTE)

You must demonstrate a genuine intention to study in Australia and return to your home country after completing your studies. This can be shown through evidence of strong ties to your home country, clear academic goals, and sufficient financial support.

Health Coverage for Overseas Students (OSHC)

You are also required to have valid health insurance for the entire duration of your stay in Australia.

Fulfill the conditions for character and health

In addition, you must fulfill character requirements and pass health exams.

Documentation:

  •  Valid Passport
  •  Confirmation of Enrolment
  •  Proof of Financial Capacity
  •  Completed Application Form
  •  Proof of English Language Proficiency
  •  Health Insurance
