Canada

Canada consistently welcomes international students and is home to a wide range of globally recognized educational institutions. According to the Higher Education 2020 rankings, seven Canadian universities are listed among the world’s top 200. In 2018, more than 570,000 international students were enrolled in Canadian institutions, with the majority coming from countries such as India, China, South Korea, and France. This growing trend has led to a significant rise in the number of Indian students choosing Canada for their higher education, with a 16% increase in 2018 alone.

Education System in Canada

Canada’s education system is globally respected for its high academic standards and innovative teaching methods. It offers a wide range of programs at colleges and universities, including diplomas, bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees. Institutions emphasize research, practical learning, and multicultural inclusion, making it an ideal destination for international students.

  1. Universities
    • Canadian universities mainly offer degree programs at both undergraduate and postgraduate levels. Alongside these, they also provide postgraduate diplomas and certificates, typically lasting up to one year. Canada is a leading choice for international students, with India ranking among the top two countries contributing to student enrolment.
  2. Colleges
    • Colleges in Canada, often known as community colleges, institutes of technology, or colleges of applied arts, specialize in applied technical education. They provide practical, hands-on training across a wide range of technical and vocational fields.

Cost of Studying in Canada

Compared to other top study destinations like the USA and the UK, studying in Canada is generally more cost-effective. The average tuition for a master’s degree ranges from CAD 13,000 to CAD 66,000, while undergraduate programs typically cost between CAD 20,000 and CAD 56,000, depending on the university and course.

Canada Visa Process for Students

Getting a student visa to study abroad is generally a straightforward process. In Canada, study permits are issued by Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) and are valid for the length of your academic program. Our Canada study consultants have outlined the key steps to guide you through the process of obtaining a Canadian student visa.

 Apply for a study permit
 Pay the application fee of CAD 150
 Gather the required documents and upload them

If you’re planning to study in Canada for less than six months, a study permit is not required. For further information about Canadian universities, feel free to contact our Canada study abroad experts at Student Bridge.
